1. Research Question

Among Medicare fee-for-service beneficiaries diagnosed with non-metastatic oropharyngeal squamous cell carcinoma (OPSCC), how does TORS (transoral robotic surgery) compare to radiation-based therapy with respect to overall survival and functional morbidity (dysphagia, gastrostomy tube, tracheostomy)? Two clinically distinct comparisons are made:

Comparison A TORS alone vs RT alone

TORS alone: patients who received TORS within 12 months of diagnosis — no chemotherapy and no radiation in the same window.

RT alone: patients who received radiation within 12 months of diagnosis — no chemotherapy and no TORS.

Clinical rationale: Compares primary surgical resection to definitive radiation as single-modality treatments for early/favorable OPSCC.

Comparison B TORS + RT vs CT/CRT

TORS + RT: patients who received TORS followed by adjuvant radiation — no chemotherapy in the same window.

CT/CRT: patients who received both chemotherapy and radiation — no TORS in the same window.

Clinical rationale: Compares TORS-based multimodal therapy (surgery + adjuvant RT) to definitive concurrent chemoradiation for locally advanced OPSCC.

3.2 HNC Diagnosis Confirmation

Raw HNC diagnosis claims are extracted from all three claim sources (inpatient, outpatient, carrier) for ICD-10 prefixes C00–C14 and C30–C32. The confirmation algorithm (hnc_confirmed.sql) requires:

A At least one inpatient claim with HNC as principal diagnosis, or
B At least two outpatient/carrier claims with HNC diagnosis, spaced ≥30 days apart

This mirrors the Warren-type algorithm for cancer case ascertainment in claims data. The earliest qualifying claim date becomes first_hnc_date.

3.3 OPSCC Identification

From the confirmed HNC universe, patients are restricted to those with at least one raw diagnosis claim at an OPSCC primary site:

ICD-10 PrefixSite
C01Base of tongue
C09Tonsil
C10Oropharynx (other and unspecified)
C14Lip, oral cavity, and pharynx — ill-defined sites (overlapping)

3.4 Enrollment & Eligibility

1 Continuous FFS Medicare enrollment
Must have 6 consecutive months of fee-for-service (FFS) enrollment beginning with the month of first_hnc_date.
FFS month defined as: HMOIND = '0' (no HMO) AND MDCR_STUS_CD IN ('10','11','20','21') (Part A+B).

3.5 Exclusion Criteria

X1 Metastatic disease
Any diagnosis of C76%, C77%, C78%, or C79% in any claim within ±90 days of first_hnc_date. Applied at the opscc_propensity.sql step (has_metastatic_dx = FALSE).
X2 Treatment group = "Other"
Patients who do not fit any of the four protocol-defined groups (e.g., TORS + chemotherapy without radiation, chemotherapy alone, TORS + chemotherapy + radiation) are assigned tx_group = 'Other' and excluded from both PSM analyses. They are retained in opscc_propensity for descriptive purposes.
X4 C77 (nodal disease) at time of outcomes analysis
Patients with any C77% code in inpatient/outpatient/carrier claims are excluded from functional outcome OR analysis and subgroup survival analysis. Applied at the Python analysis step.

5. Comorbidity Scoring

Elixhauser comorbidity flags are computed via opscc_elixhauser.sql using the Quan et al. ICD-10-CM adaptation (see comorbidity.py for code lists). The lookback window is 12 months before first_hnc_date (exclusive), applied across all three claim sources. Index HNC codes are excluded by the lookback window.

The van Walraven summary score is computed from the 31 Elixhauser flags using the weights from van Walraven et al. (2009). It is used as a continuous covariate in the propensity model and as a stratification variable (tertiles) in subgroup analyses.

Implemented in iptw_analysis.py using sklearn.linear_model.LogisticRegression. Two independent PSM runs are performed — one per comparison — via a shared run_psm() function.

All downstream analysis scripts filter opscc_propensity by psm_matched_A = TRUE or psm_matched_B = TRUE to select the appropriate matched cohort for each comparison.

7.4 Pre-existing Outcome Exclusion

For each patient and each functional outcome, any code appearing before first_tx_date (going back to first_tx_date − 365 days) sets that outcome field to NULL for that patient. This excludes pre-existing conditions from the outcome analysis but retains the patient in the cohort. Applied in opscc_outcomes.sql via the pre_existing boolean flag.

8. Statistical Analysis

All analyses loop over COMPARISONS = [('A', 'psm_matched_A', ...), ('B', 'psm_matched_B', ...)]. Each comparison filters the matched cohort independently before analysis.

Survival Analysis (survival_analysis.py)

  • Kaplan-Meier: Overall survival curves per comparison (TORS arm vs control arm). Log-rank test for group difference.
  • Cox PH: Adjusted for age and van Walraven score. Stratified by PSM match ID. HR < 1 favors the TORS arm of each comparison.
  • Reported statistics: Median survival, 1-year/3-year/5-year survival probability, HR (95% CI), log-rank p-value.

Functional Outcomes Analysis (outcomes_analysis.py)

  • Odds ratios computed from 2×2 contingency tables at each timepoint.
  • Chi-square test for independence (scipy.stats.chi2_contingency).
  • Pre-existing conditions excluded (NULL outcomes not counted in denominator).
  • Reference group: control arm of each comparison (RT alone for Comp A; CT/CRT for Comp B). OR < 1 favors the TORS arm.
  • C77 nodal disease patients excluded from functional outcome analyses.

11. Key Design Decisions

DecisionRationale
Diagnosis cutoff: June 30, 2023Outpatient revenue file (out_revenuek_all) only available through June 2023; extending beyond this date would leave outpatient-billed RT/chemo codes undetected in H2 2023. Carrier and inpatient files are complete through December 2023.
12-month treatment windowCaptures planned multimodal treatment sequences while excluding late salvage therapy
Warren-type HNC confirmationReduces false positives from incidental or rule-out diagnosis codes in outpatient claims
±90-day metastatic exclusion windowDistinguishes de novo metastatic disease from late recurrence while allowing adequate staging workup time
Pre-existing outcome exclusion (not patient exclusion)Retains sample size; nulling the outcome for pre-existing patients avoids attributing prevalent disease to treatment
Logit PS caliper at 0.2 × SDStandard recommended caliper (Austin 2011); balances match quality vs. sample retention
CT/CRT group requires both chemo AND radiationDistinguishes definitive chemoradiation from RT alone or chemo alone; chemo and RT dates stored separately to enable four-group classification
Two independent PSM runs (A and B)Comp A and Comp B have distinct patient populations (RT-naive vs chemo-naive); a single matched cohort cannot serve both comparisons
C77 nodal exclusion for functional outcomesNodal disease substantially increases risk of G-tube and trach independent of treatment; confounds functional outcome comparisons
Van Walraven score used for subgroup stratificationProvides a validated single-number summary of Elixhauser comorbidity burden suitable for tertile-based subgrouping
